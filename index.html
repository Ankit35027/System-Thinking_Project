Problem Statement:-

Technology Addiction Among Teen Agers.

Aim:-

The aim of this project is to apply systems thinking to deeply understand and address the complex issue of digital addiction among teenagers in India. As technology becomes increasingly embedded in everyday life, especially among the youth, excessive screen time and dependency on digital devices have begun to significantly affect teenagers' mental health, academic performance, physical well-being, and social relationships.

Through this project, we aim to move beyond surface-level observations and explore the underlying structures, interdependencies, and feedback mechanisms that contribute to digital addiction. This involves identifying not just the visible symptoms‚Äîsuch as decreased attention span, sleep disturbances, and social isolation‚Äîbut also the root causes and reinforcing loops, such as peer influence, algorithm-driven content, lack of parental awareness, inadequate digital education, and socio-economic drivers.

Using systems thinking tools such as Causal Loop Diagrams (CLDs), Stock and Flow Diagrams, and Behavior Over Time Graphs, we will map the system to uncover how various factors interact over time. We will also conduct stakeholder analysis to understand the roles of parents, educators, policymakers, tech companies, and the teenagers themselves in shaping the digital environment.

By embracing a holistic and dynamic approach, our goal is not only to mitigate the immediate effects of digital addiction but to influence the system in ways that prevent its recurrence, promote digital well-being, and empower teenagers to use technology mindfully and responsibly.
